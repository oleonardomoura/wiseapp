Nível A2 - Unidade 26: The Best Choice

Título da Cena: The Award (A Premiação)

Foco Gramatical: Superlativos

Vocabulário: Recordes e Opiniões

1. TEXTO FLUÍDO - EN

It is Friday night. The living room is decorated with gold balloons. Matt is wearing a tuxedo (it is a little small for him). He holds a golden cup.

"Welcome to the first annual Apartment 20 Awards!" Matt announces. "Tonight, we honor the best people in the world... us."

David sits straight on the sofa. "This is the most ridiculous idea ever," he whispers to Lisa.

"And the first award goes to David!" Matt shouts. "David is the most organized person in New York. His socks are the neatest socks in history."

David stands up, surprisingly happy. "Thank you. Organization is the most important key to success."

"Next," Matt continues. "The award for the loudest laugh goes to... Jennifer!"

Jennifer laughs loudly. "That is true! I am the happiest person here."

Matt looks at Matthew in the corner. "And Matthew wins the award for the quietest neighbor. He is also the most mysterious man I know."

Matthew nods once. "Thank you. Silence is the best sound."

"And finally," Matt pauses for dramatic effect. "The award for the most handsome, the funniest, and the most talented man goes to... Me!"

Courteney shakes her head. "Matt, you are the worst judge ever. But you are definitely the craziest roommate."

Matt hugs his golden cup. "Thank you, Courteney. Being the craziest is a record too."

4. ANÁLISE BILÍNGUE (SENTENCE BY SENTENCE)

- Tuxedo. | Smoking (Roupa formal masculina).

- Neatest. | O mais arrumado / O mais impecável (Vem de "Neat").

- Judge. | Juiz.

- Dramatic effect. | Efeito dramático.

- Ever. | De todos os tempos / De todos (Usado com superlativos para exagero: "The worst ever").

- Record. | Recorde.

5. TOOLKIT GRAMATICAL (DICAS)

SUPERLATIVOS (O MAIS... DE TODOS):

Usamos para destacar UM em um grupo. É o "topo" da lista.

A REGRA DO "THE" (O Artigo Obrigatório):

Sempre use THE antes do superlativo.

1. ADJETIVOS CURTOS:

Adicione "EST" no final.

- Old -> The oldest (O mais velho).

- Tall -> The tallest (O mais alto).

- Crazy -> The craziest (O mais louco - Y vira I).

- Quiet -> The quietest (O mais quieto).

2. ADJETIVOS LONGOS:

Use "THE MOST" antes da palavra.

- Organized -> The most organized.

- Ridiculous -> The most ridiculous.

- Mysterious -> The most mysterious.

3. IRREGULARES (MEMORIZE!):

- Good (Bom) -> The BEST (O melhor).

- Bad (Ruim) -> The WORST (O pior).

6. VOCABULÁRIO ESSENCIAL

- Best: Melhor.

- Worst: Pior.

- Most: Mais (quantidade/intensidade).

- Award: Prêmio.

- Cup / Trophy: Taça / Troféu.

- Loud: Alto (som).

- Quiet: Quieto / Silencioso.

- Handsome: Bonito (homem).

- Crazy: Louco.

- Funny: Engraçado.
